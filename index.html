Hey — I'm Vansh, but everyone calls me Ru. I'm a 3D generalist most at home in environment design and retro-PSX texture work, but the through-line of everything I do is the same: I like sitting at the intersection of building, breaking, and understanding.

On the craft side that's Unreal Engine, Blueprints, C++, Python, Blender, mocap and modding — environments, characters, tools. Off-screen the same instinct turns into hardware, network design, server management and IT — the quieter layer that lets the creative one actually run.

The reason it all sits together: psychology & neuroscience are the soft science I read like other people read fiction, and I lucid dream often enough that the imagery from those nights feeds straight back into the renders. Build a world, then build the system that holds it up. That's the whole job.

PRJ — 001 / Environment

The town that cracked open.

Environment-concept assignment: take a multiplayer map from a pre-2011 shooter and rebuild it under a new theme. I pulled the suburban shell of the original and grew something through it — a bone-dry cult town with a tree the size of the house erupting from inside, half-built mannequins watching the road, and a sign that reads less like a welcome and more like a warning.

PRJ — 002 / Render

A room I visited in sleep.

One of an ongoing series of renders excavated directly from lucid dreams. Two chairs facing a hole-in-the-wall ocean, sand creeping inward, a guitar in the dust. I rebuild the geometry from memory as soon as I'm awake — fidelity to feeling first, accuracy second. The light, the proportions and the warmth are exactly as I remember them.
